About NJIT


In 1881, the Newark College of Engineering was founded to give young minds a way to further their education. As the college grew it began to specialize in more technical aspects of education, and as a result, gained a new name. In September of 1974 the university changed its name to New Jersey Institute of Technology, also known as NJIT. NJIT is know as a public polytechnic university. Located in Newark NJIT offers six specialized colleges:

  • Newark College of Engineering (1919)
  • Hillier College of Architecture and Design (1973)
  • College of Science and Liberal Arts (1982)
  • Martin Tuchman School of Management (1988)
  • Albert Dorman Honors College (1994)
  • Ying Wu College of Computing (2001)



Statistics

NJIT is rated “Very High” for Racial Demographics & Diversity, 67% percent of students are minorities or people of color (BIPOC). In addition most NJIT studnts are aged 22-24 with students age 20-21 coming in at a close 2nd. As for its gender split, it sits at a 26.2% Female and 73.8% Male split.Finally NJIT's average GPA sits at a 3.59, making it farly competive when dealing with GPAs.
